Pass 1 — Skim: What's the paper about?

Before 2017, the best translation and text-generation models used RNNs (Recurrent Neural Networks) — they read text one word at a time, like reading left-to-right with a finger on the page.

Analogy: Imagine a doctor reading a radiology report one word at a time and forgetting the beginning by the time they reach the end. That's an RNN's problem with long sequences.

Vaswani et al. asked: "What if we throw away RNNs entirely and use only attention?"

  • They introduced the Transformer — a new architecture built solely on attention.
  • It processes all words in parallel (like reading the whole page at once), making it dramatically faster.
  • It beat every existing model on translation benchmarks, measured by BLEU scores.
  • Hence the title: "Attention Is All You Need."
Why you care at KHCC: GPT-4, BERT, BioBERT, ClinicalBERT — every model you'll use this session — is a Transformer descendant. Understanding this paper means understanding the engine under the hood.

Pass 2 — Key Ideas: Old Way vs New Way

The Old Way (RNNs)

  • Sequential — one word at a time
  • Slow on GPUs/TPUs
  • Forgets long-range context
  • Hard to parallelise

The New Way (Transformer)

  • Parallel — all words at once
  • Massively faster training
  • Captures long-range dependencies
  • Uses attention instead of recurrence

Self-Attention — The Star of the Show

Self-attention lets every word look at every other word in the sentence to decide which ones matter most for understanding context.

Analogy: Imagine a tumor board meeting. Each specialist (word) can ask every other specialist: "How relevant are you to my part of the case?" The answers determine how much weight each opinion gets. That's attention.

The math boils down to three matrices:

  • Q (Query) — "What am I looking for?"
  • K (Key) — "What do I contain?"
  • V (Value) — "What information do I give back?"
Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(Q · Kᵀ / √dₖ) · V

The √dₖ scaling prevents the dot products from getting too large (which would push softmax into regions with tiny gradients).

Multi-Head Attention

Instead of one attention calculation, the Transformer runs 8 parallel "heads" — each learns a different "perspective."

Analogy: 8 radiologists each reading the same CT scan but focusing on different things — one on lung nodules, another on mediastinal lymph nodes, another on bone lesions. Their combined read is far richer than any single opinion.

Pass 3 — Architecture Deep Dive

The Transformer has an Encoder (reads the input) and a Decoder (generates the output). Both are stacks of 6 identical layers.

Sublayer output = LayerNorm(x + Sublayer(x))

x + Sublayer(x) is a residual connection (add the input back so gradients flow smoothly). LayerNorm stabilises training.

Other essential components

  • Embeddings — convert words to number vectors the model can process.
  • Positional Encoding — since attention sees all words at once (no order!), this adds position information so the model knows word order.
  • Softmax output — the final layer predicts the probability of each possible next word.
Clinical parallel: Positional encoding is like the date/time stamp on lab results. Without it, the model can't tell whether a hemoglobin of 7 was the first or last reading — context that changes the clinical decision entirely.

What you learned

  • Pass 1: The Transformer replaced RNNs with pure attention, enabling parallel processing and beating SOTA translation benchmarks.
  • Pass 2: Self-attention uses Q/K/V matrices; multi-head attention runs 8 parallel perspectives; this is what powers every model on HuggingFace.
  • Pass 3: The Encoder has 2 sublayers (self-attention + FFN); the Decoder has 3 (masked self-attention + cross-attention + FFN); residual connections and LayerNorm keep training stable.
